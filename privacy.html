Privacy Policy — PlaneFlow

Last updated: 2 July 2026

PlaneFlow (“the game”, “we”) is a casual air-traffic-control game. This policy explains what data the game handles and how. We keep it minimal on purpose: the game asks for no personal information and has no account of its own. On Android, the game optionally lets you sign in with Google Play Games to use achievements and leaderboards — that part is described below and is entirely optional.

What we store

The game stores the following locally on your device (in the browser/app storage), so your progress and preferences persist between sessions:

  • Game progress & settings — unlocked levels, stars, best results, chosen language, sound/vibration and visual-theme preferences.
  • Anonymous gameplay analytics — a randomly generated identifier (not linked to you or any account), session timing, and in-game events such as level start/finish, tutorial steps, settings changes, and error/crash details. Alongside each event we store basic technical context: app version, interface language, platform, and screen size. On the web this stays on your device; in the Android app, once you consent, it is also sent to Firebase (see below).

Google Play Games (Android, optional)

The Android version of PlaneFlow offers an optional sign-in with Google Play Games so you can earn achievements and post Survival scores to leaderboards. This feature is provided by Google. It is off until you choose to sign in, and you can play the entire game without it. The web version does not use Google Play Games at all.

When you sign in, Google provides the game with your Play Games player ID and your Play Games display name (gamer tag) and avatar, and the game sends your achievement progress and leaderboard scores to Google Play Games Services. Your gamer tag and scores are stored on Google’s servers and can be visible to other players on the leaderboards you take part in. We use this data only to run achievements and leaderboards — we do not use it for advertising and we do not sell it.

This Play Games data is collected and processed by Google under Google’s Privacy Policy, and you control it through your Google account and your Play Games settings (including your gamer-tag visibility and deleting your Play Games data). If you never sign in, no Play Games data leaves your device.

Analytics & diagnostics — Firebase (Android app, with your consent)

The Android app uses Google’s Firebase for anonymous analytics (Google Analytics for Firebase), crash reporting (Crashlytics) and performance monitoring (Performance Monitoring). This collection is off by default and starts only after you agree on the consent screen; if you decline, nothing is sent. The web version does not use Firebase.

When enabled, Firebase collects the anonymous gameplay events and technical context described above, crash and performance diagnostics, a Firebase installation identifier, and — for analytics attribution — your device’s advertising ID. This data is not linked to your identity, is used only to understand aggregate usage and fix problems, is not shared with third parties for their own purposes, and is never sold. It is processed by Google under Google’s Privacy Policy. You can stop it at any time by declining consent or clearing the app’s data. No ads are shown in the game.

What we do NOT collect

  • No name, email, phone number, or any data that identifies you personally (your Play Games gamer tag is a public alias you choose in Google Play Games, not collected by us as your identity).
  • No precise location, contacts, photos, microphone or camera access.
  • No ads are shown in the game, and we never sell your data to anyone.

Where the data goes

Game progress and settings stay on your device and are not transmitted to any server. Data that leaves your device is limited to: (1) the Google Play Games information above, only if you sign in; and (2) in the Android app, the Firebase analytics and diagnostics described above, only after you consent. On the web, analytics stay on your device. We use analytics only to improve the game and to understand aggregate, anonymous usage (for example, how many players complete the tutorial). We never sell your data.

Children

PlaneFlow is suitable for a general audience and is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children.

Data retention & your control

Because the on-device data is stored on your device, you can erase all of it at any time by clearing the app’s data/storage or uninstalling the game. If you signed in with Google Play Games, your achievements and leaderboard scores live in Google Play Games; you can manage or delete them through your Play Games settings. PlaneFlow itself has no account to delete.

Security

The game is served over HTTPS. Any data transmitted off your device — Google Play Games sign-in data and the Firebase analytics/diagnostics above — is sent over encrypted connections.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, we will update the “Last updated” date above and, where required, the Google Play listing.
