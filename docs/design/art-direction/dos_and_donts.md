# PlaneFlow — Do's & Don'ts

> Fast guardrails. When in doubt, re-read [`visual_pillars.md`](visual_pillars.md).
> Parent: [`art_direction_v1.md`](art_direction_v1.md).

---

## ✅ Do

**Readability**
- Keep aircraft silhouettes oversized and instantly recognizable.
- Keep routes thick, glowing, high-contrast, and always visible.
- Back up route color with a second channel (dash / arrow / icon).
- Maintain a clear information hierarchy.

**Atmosphere**
- Add small living moments (idle vehicles, red carpet, snowplow at work).
- Use warm lamp glow at night for cozy mood.
- Keep the world cute and alive.

**Style**
- Simplified shapes, clean silhouettes, soft shadows, stylized proportions.
- Warm, muted palette (max 6–7 dominant colors).
- Smooth, soft, satisfying, non-chaotic animation.

**UI**
- Icon-first, large touch targets, rounded warm cards.
- One primary CTA per panel.

**Performance**
- Design for mid-range phones first.

---

## ❌ Don't

**Realism creep**
- ✘ Photorealistic airports.
- ✘ Realistic scale.
- ✘ Overly detailed environments.
- ✘ Tiny unreadable props or planes.

**Visual noise**
- ✘ Cluttered UI / dense text.
- ✘ Excessive or heavy particle effects.
- ✘ Aggressive flashing or strobing.
- ✘ Color encoded as the *only* channel for a route state.

**Mood breakers**
- ✘ Dark, unreadable scenes.
- ✘ Aggressive / oversaturated colors.
- ✘ Stressful, high-friction UX.

**Camera**
- ✘ Isometric or angled gameplay camera. Gameplay is **top-down only**.
  (The reference image's tilt is presentation-only — see
  [`art_direction_v1.md`](art_direction_v1.md#camera).)

**Performance**
- ✘ Effects that won't hold framerate on mid-range phones.

---

## The one rule that breaks ties

> **Readability First.** If a beautiful choice hurts gameplay clarity, the choice is wrong.
