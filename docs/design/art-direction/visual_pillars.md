# PlaneFlow — Visual Pillars

> The four pillars every visual decision must serve. If a choice helps one pillar but
> breaks another, **Readability First wins**. Parent doc: [`art_direction_v1.md`](art_direction_v1.md).

The shipped style-guide condenses these into a checklist (panel 13, "Visual Notes"):
*highly readable · clear silhouettes · warm and inviting · soft shadows · satisfying
animations · simple shapes · calm, not stressful · cute and alive world · small stories
everywhere · gameplay clarity first.*

---

## 1. Readability First

Gameplay clarity ALWAYS wins over realism.

Rules:

- Routes must be instantly readable.
- Aircraft silhouettes must be recognizable in **< 0.5 seconds**.
- Important interactions must stay visible **while objects are moving**.
- The player's finger must not hide critical information.

Never sacrifice gameplay clarity for visual realism. This is the tie-breaker pillar.

---

## 2. Warm Atmosphere

The airport should feel alive and emotionally pleasant.

Mood:

- warm lights at night
- tiny moving service vehicles
- subtle airport activity
- small environmental storytelling moments

Storytelling examples (small stories everywhere):

- ambulance waiting near the terminal
- limo with red carpet for a VIP jet
- snowplow cleaning the runway
- de-icing trucks spraying aircraft
- beavers chewing a fallen tree
- maintenance crews repairing damage

The world should feel cute and alive.

---

## 3. Cognitive Flow

The game must support a "brain occupied" flow state.

Visual rules:

- no visual overload
- clean information hierarchy
- smooth route drawing
- low UI friction
- calming movement patterns

Animations should feel **smooth, soft, satisfying, non-chaotic**. No aggressive
flashing. No visual noise.

---

## 4. Stylized, Not Realistic

**Avoid:** photorealism, realistic airport complexity, excessive details, tiny
unreadable props.

**Prefer:** simplified shapes, clean silhouettes, stylized proportions, soft shadows,
visual warmth.

- Aircraft should be slightly oversized for readability.
- Ground vehicles should be cute and visually distinct.

---

## Quick self-check (apply to any new asset)

1. Can a player identify what this is in under half a second?
2. Does it stay readable while moving and under a finger?
3. Does it add warmth/life without adding clutter?
4. Is it simplified, not detailed-for-detail's-sake?
5. Will it run cheaply on a mid-range phone?

If any answer is "no", simplify before shipping.
